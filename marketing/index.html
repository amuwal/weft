Weft is one small thing,
so it can be a good one.

It's easier to keep an app calm by being honest about everything it chose not to be. A short list of nos, so you know what you're holding.

Not a CRM

No pipelines, no stages, no lead status. The market already has fifty of those — and they all feel like work.

Not a contact app

Apple Contacts already holds phone numbers and addresses. Weft holds memory — the things you'd otherwise forget.

Not a checklist

No little flame icons, no chains to keep alive, no guilt when you skip a week. Friendship isn't a workout plan.

Not a journal of you

Day One already exists for that, and it's wonderful. Weft is a journal about other people, written from your side.

Not AI-powered

No cloud LLM ever sees your notes. No auto-generated reminders. The whole point is that you wrote it.

Not on the web

iOS only, with a small Watch app, a widget, and an iPad layout. We chose depth over reach — Android can wait.

Calm by design

Small things, done with care.

Everything Weft ships is here because it earned its place. Nothing else.

Gentle rhythm

Pick weekly, biweekly, monthly, or quarterly for each person. The pace bends to the relationship — not the other way around.

Threads, not tasks

Mark a thought as "follow up" and it comes back on its date, folded softly into Today. No checkbox guilt.

iCloud, your way

Off by default. Switch it on for end-to-end-encrypted sync across iPhone, iPad, and Watch — never anyone else's server.

Today, on your wrist

A small Watch face shows the one or two people on your mind. A tap caught-them-up; no scrolling, no chrome.

Export, always

One tap to PDF or Markdown. Your words are yours; the app is just a place to keep them. Leaving should never be sticky.

Designed for iOS 26

Native Liquid Glass, Dynamic Type, Apple Watch, lock-screen widgets, and Apple Shortcuts. Feels like part of the OS, because it is.

Background

What's a personal CRM, and why a quiet one?

A short explainer for everyone who arrived here searching for a relationship app and wondered if Weft was the right fit.

What "personal CRM" actually means

A personal CRM is software for people, not for sales pipelines. Where a workplace CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot) tracks leads and deals through a funnel, a personal CRM is a small private space for remembering the humans in your own life — birthdays, last conversations, kids' names, the worry someone mentioned in passing.

The category emerged because contact apps are too thin (just phone numbers) and journals are too self-focused (a diary about you, not them). A personal CRM holds the middle ground: per-person memory, gentle rhythm, no public profile.

Who Weft is for

Weft was built for people who want to stay close to a small, defined circle — not network with thousands. If any of these sound like you, you're the audience:

  • The senior engineer who keeps forgetting their coworker's kid's name and feels bad each time.
  • The new parent watching close friendships erode under the weight of admin and exhaustion.
  • The freelancer who wants to remember which client said what, without standing up a CRM.
  • The introvert who loves a small handful of people and wants a private place to think about them.
  • The adult child who lives far from family and wants to call their mom on the right week, not the wrong month.
  • Anyone who tried Notion templates or a spreadsheet and bounced off the overhead.

How Weft differs from Dex, Clay, Folk, and other personal CRMs

The personal CRM market splits into two patterns. Networker tools like Dex and Clay are designed for thousands of contacts, LinkedIn-style enrichment, and broadcast follow-ups — useful if you're a founder building a Rolodex, less so if you just want to remember your closest twenty people. Workplace CRM spillover like Folk CRM repackages sales pipelines for personal use; pipelines feel like work even when relabeled.

Weft sits in a third lane. The cap is intentional (five to twenty-five people). The vocabulary is borrowed from journaling, not from sales — notes, threads, rhythm — never "lead," "stage," "engagement." Nothing is shared, nothing is public, nothing is auto-summarized by AI. The whole product is sized around a single idea: that the small circle deserves a calmer, more private surface than the networker apps offer.

If you want auto-pulled contact feeds, LinkedIn integration, or shared workspaces, those products are excellent at it. If you want a quiet, on-device notebook that remembers what you can't, that's Weft.

Why iOS-only, why iOS 26

Weft is built for iOS 26 specifically — native Liquid Glass surfaces, Apple Watch face, lock-screen widgets, Apple Shortcuts, end-to-end-encrypted CloudKit sync. We chose depth over reach: an app that feels like part of the operating system instead of a cross-platform shell that feels like part of none.

Practically: no Android, no web app, no desktop client. Your private notes stay in your iCloud private database, never on a Weft server (because there is no Weft server).
