Weft is one small thing,
so it can be a good one.

It's easier to keep an app calm by being honest about everything it chose not to be. A short list of nos, so you know what you're holding.

Not a CRM

No pipelines, no stages, no lead status. The market already has fifty of those — and they all feel like work.

Not a contact app

Apple Contacts already holds phone numbers and addresses. Weft holds memory — the things you'd otherwise forget.

Not a checklist

No little flame icons, no chains to keep alive, no guilt when you skip a week. Friendship isn't a workout plan.

Not a journal of you

Day One already exists for that, and it's wonderful. Weft is a journal about other people, written from your side.

Not AI-powered

No cloud LLM ever sees your notes. No auto-generated reminders. The whole point is that you wrote it.

Not on the web

iOS only, with a small Watch app, a widget, and an iPad layout. We chose depth over reach — Android can wait.

Calm by design

Small things, done with care.

Everything Weft ships is here because it earned its place. Nothing else.

Gentle rhythm

Pick weekly, biweekly, monthly, or quarterly for each person. The pace bends to the relationship — not the other way around.

Threads, not tasks

Mark a thought as "follow up" and it comes back on its date, folded softly into Today. No checkbox guilt.

iCloud, your way

Off by default. Switch it on for end-to-end-encrypted sync across iPhone, iPad, and Watch — never anyone else's server.

Today, on your wrist

A small Watch face shows the one or two people on your mind. A tap caught-them-up; no scrolling, no chrome.

Export, always

One tap to PDF or Markdown. Your words are yours; the app is just a place to keep them. Leaving should never be sticky.

Designed for iOS 26

Native Liquid Glass, Dynamic Type, Apple Watch, lock-screen widgets, and Apple Shortcuts. Feels like part of the OS, because it is.

Honest answers

Things people ask.

Is my data ever sent to a server?
By default, no. Weft writes to your phone's local database — that's it. If you switch on iCloud sync, Apple's private database encrypts each note end-to-end with your iCloud key. Weft has no servers, no analytics endpoint, and no third-party SDKs that phone home.
Why no AI?
Because the value is in you writing the sentence. An AI that auto-summarizes your last text exchange may be technically possible, but it'd hollow the app out. v2 may quietly use on-device Apple Intelligence for tiny things — never to write notes for you.
Will there be an Android version? A web version?
Not for a long time. Weft is built around iOS 26 features — Liquid Glass, Apple Watch, widgets, Apple Shortcuts. Cross-platform would water it down. If you live on Android, we'd rather you use a different app than a worse Weft.
What happens if I stop paying?
Your data stays. The app reverts to the free tier limits — seven people visible, sync paused — but you keep every note ever written, and you can export the lot to PDF or Markdown with one tap. Subscriptions buy features, not your words.
Who built it?
One developer, working out of Tokyo. Weft is a deliberate, single-person project — the kind of app you can email about and hear back from the same person who wrote the code. Say hello.
What about import from Notion / Google Contacts / a CSV?
v1 imports from Apple Contacts only, and only the names you pick. CSV / Notion / Google import are on the maybe pile for v1.1 — but Weft is intentionally small, and most people who try to import a thousand contacts realize halfway in that they meant fifteen.
