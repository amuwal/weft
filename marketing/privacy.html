Privacy, in plain English.

The short version: Weft writes to your phone. It doesn't write to us. If you turn on iCloud, your notes go to Apple — and even Apple can't read them.

One paragraph summary. Weft has no servers. We do not collect, store, or transmit any data about you or the people in your notes. The app runs entirely on your device. iCloud sync, if you enable it, uses Apple's end-to-end encrypted private database — Weft never sees the contents.

What we collect

Nothing on our side. Weft has no analytics SDK, no crash-reporting service, no advertising identifiers, and no third-party telemetry of any kind.

The data you create — names, notes, threads, photos you attach, rhythm settings — lives in a SwiftData store on your device's local file system. Standard iOS sandboxing applies: no other app can read it.

iCloud sync (optional)

If you toggle iCloud sync on, Weft writes to your iCloud private database. Apple encrypts this with your iCloud key. Apple does not read it; neither do we. We have no way to access it because we have no servers in the loop.

If you sign out of iCloud, Apple removes the local copy of synced data and our app falls back to its local SwiftData store on that device.

Apple's role

If you bought a subscription, Apple processes the payment under their privacy policy. We see a tokenized identifier saying "this device has an active Premium subscription." We do not see your name, email, billing address, or card number.

The App Store also publishes aggregate, anonymized download counts on our App Store Connect dashboard. We can see "X people in Japan installed Weft this week" — never who, never which devices.

What we don't do

  • No advertising — anywhere, ever. Weft does not sell ads or share data with ad networks.
  • No selling, renting, or licensing data — to anyone, for any purpose.
  • No "anonymized analytics" sent to a third-party service.
  • No AI model is trained on your notes. Weft v1 has no AI, and v2 will only use on-device Apple Intelligence — which stays on your phone.
  • No public profile, no social graph, no leaderboard.

Your control

You can export every note to PDF or Markdown with one tap from Settings → Data. Leaving Weft should never be sticky.

You can also tap "Delete all data" in Settings to wipe the local store and disable sync. This is irreversible and we can't help you recover anything — because we never had it.

Children's privacy

Weft is rated 4+ in the App Store, but the app's purpose — keeping notes about people in your life — is intended for users 13 years and older. We don't knowingly collect data from anyone, so there's nothing to delete if a younger user installs the app.

Changes to this policy

If we change anything, we'll update the date at the top and note what changed in the app's release notes. We won't expand data collection without a clear, opt-in change you can refuse.

Contact

Questions or worries? Write to hello@getweft.xyz. A real person — the developer — reads every email.
