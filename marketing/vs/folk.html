Weft vs Folk CRM

Folk is excellent at what it does. What it does is sales. If you're shopping for a personal CRM and Folk keeps showing up in your search results, here's the honest distinction.

The one-line answer. Folk CRM is a sales CRM with a softer aesthetic — pipelines, deals, teams, integrations. Weft is a personal journal app for the 5–25 people who matter to you. They share the letters "CRM" and almost nothing else.

The category mismatch

Folk (folk.app) is genuinely a great workplace CRM. It has team workspaces, deal pipelines, contact enrichment, mass email sequences, and the kind of integration ecosystem you need if you're running outbound sales for an agency or a startup. The product is well-designed and the team is well-funded. If you're a founder, an agency, or a consultant with a sales motion, Folk is one of the better choices.

Weft is a different category of product entirely. There are no pipelines, no deals, no teams, no email sequences. The unit isn't a "contact" with a stage; it's a "person you care about" with a memory. The vocabulary is borrowed from journaling — notes, threads, rhythm — not from sales. There's no concept of "sequencing a follow-up to your mom" because that would feel disgusting, and the product refuses to enable it.

People sometimes try to use Folk for personal relationships. It works, the way using a spreadsheet works — the structure exists but it leaks the wrong feeling. Friendship isn't a deal stage.

When Folk is the right answer

If you are running any kind of sales motion — agency outreach, freelance prospecting, fundraising, founder networking, partnerships — Folk is one of the better personal-feeling CRMs designed for that work. The integrations (LinkedIn, Gmail, WhatsApp, Slack) and the pipeline view exist for real reasons, and they pay back the price.

If your problem statement involves the word "lead" or "deal" or "opportunity," install Folk. Weft would actively get in your way.

When Weft is the right answer

If your problem statement is closer to "I keep forgetting my friend's mom's surgery was last Tuesday and I meant to ask how it went" — Weft is the product. There's no pipeline. There are no leads. There's a single small list of people you care about, and a quiet place to write a sentence about each one when something happens.

You pay $18.99/year — or $39.99 once — not $216. The app fits in your pocket because that's where you'll actually use it (the gap between "I should write a note about that" and the next time you'd open a laptop is usually long enough to forget). And nobody on a sales-engineering team is reading your notes about your dad's third round of chemo, because there is no sales-engineering team, and there's no cloud either.

The honest verdict

If you've been searching for a "personal CRM" and Folk's results have been confusing — yes, it has the words on its site, but it's selling you a different product. Folk is for work. Weft is for the people you love.

  • "I need to track leads through a pipeline." → Folk CRM.
  • "I need to remember the small things about the people I love." → Weft.
