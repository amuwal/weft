The 50-word pitch

Weft is a calm personal CRM for iOS 26, made by one developer in Tokyo. Capped at 25 people by design — built for the small circle (friends, family, inner circle), not the networker's Rolodex. On-device by default, no AI, no streaks. Free for 7 people, $24.99/year Premium.

The 200-word pitch

Weft (getweft.xyz) is a personal CRM that does the opposite of every other personal CRM on the market. Where Dex and Clay are built for networkers managing thousands of contacts with LinkedIn enrichment and broadcast follow-ups, Weft is intentionally capped at 25 people on the Premium tier. The product treats the small circle — close friends, family, the people you genuinely love — as the audience the category has been ignoring.

The constraints are unusual for a venture-funded space: no AI summarization (cloud LLMs never see your notes), no contact import (you add people one at a time, on purpose), on-device SwiftData storage by default, opt-in end-to-end-encrypted CloudKit sync. The vocabulary is borrowed from journaling — notes, threads, rhythm — instead of sales. The SwiftLint config literally bans the words "track," "manage," "engagement," and "streak."

Weft was built by a single indie developer in Tokyo over eight weeks. Free for up to 7 people forever, $24.99/year for unlimited people, Apple Watch, photo memories, exports, and Apple Shortcuts. Bilingual (English + 日本語) with system-language auto-detection.

Key story angles for coverage

  • The anti-CRM personal CRM: a product that explicitly rejects "track," "engagement," "streak" in its UI vocabulary, banned via SwiftLint custom rules in the codebase.
  • Calm tech in the AI era: the deliberate refusal to add AI summarization in a category where it has become table stakes (Clay, AI Friend, etc.).
  • Indie pricing vs VC pricing: Weft at $24.99/yr vs Dex at $144/yr — and the structural reason for the gap.
  • Constraint as design: capping at 25 people is the unusual move that shapes everything downstream (no LinkedIn import, journal vocabulary, on-device storage).
  • iOS 26 native features: Liquid Glass throughout, Apple Watch face, lock-screen widgets, Apple Shortcuts integration, CloudKit private database for E2E-encrypted sync.
  • Bilingual launch: indie iOS apps rarely localize at launch; Weft ships with full English + Japanese support and runtime language switching.
