# User pain points

Drawn from app store reviews, Reddit threads, Quora, friendship-app review articles, and 2026 Google search trends.

## The core unmet need

> "I have a full calendar and steady communication with people, but my closest relationships still feel shallow. I forget what they told me last time. I miss birthdays of important people. I want to be a better friend but my memory doesn't keep up."

This is the unmet need across every "personal CRM" review thread. Loneliness searches in 2026 increasingly come from socially active people whose calendars are full but whose connections feel hollow. This is the *exact* demographic that would pay for Linger.

## Specific user complaints (across competitors)

### About networker-CRM tools (Dex, Clay, Covve)
- "Feels like a job, not a relationship"
- "Too many fields to fill out"
- "I just want to remember three things about someone, not their LinkedIn history"
- "Painfully slow" (Dex App Store reviews)
- "Overwhelming"
- "Too expensive for personal use"

### About reminder apps (Garden, Catchup, UpHabit)
- "No real depth — it nags me to text but doesn't help me remember WHAT to text about"
- "I want notes that feel like a journal, not a row in a database"
- "Reminders are too rigid — if I just saw them yesterday, don't ping me today"

### About gamified apps (Tend, Garden)
- "The garden metaphor is cute for a week then feels silly"
- "I'm not 'watering' my friends, this is condescending"
- "Streaks make me feel guilty when I miss a day, which is the opposite of what I need"

## What users say they actually want

From positive reviews and "I wish there was an app" threads:

1. **Memory** — "Help me remember what they told me, their kid's name, what they're going through"
2. **Soft prompts** — "Gentle nudges, not nagging"
3. **Privacy** — "I don't want my friend data in the cloud or training models"
4. **Tiny circle** — "I have 8 people I care about deeply, not 800"
5. **Speed** — "It should take 5 seconds to log a coffee, or I won't do it"
6. **Beauty** — "I open this app maybe 3 times a week. It should feel nice."
7. **Open loops** — "Sarah's mom is having surgery on the 14th — remind me to check in on the 15th"

## Demographic snapshot

Likely buyer:
- 25-45
- Lives in city, has more friends than time
- Already pays for Day One, Bear, Things, Calm, or similar premium utility apps
- Family-of-origin in a different city
- Genuinely values close relationships, feels guilty about not maintaining them well
- Will pay $25/yr if the app is beautiful and respects their time

Likely non-buyer:
- Sales/BD pro who wants LinkedIn integration (→ they want Dex)
- Person with <5 close people who already keeps up easily
- Teens (their relationship management happens in DMs)

## Behavioral patterns to design around

- Will open the app sporadically (not daily). Must work without retention pressure.
- Will fail to log every interaction. Must work with partial data.
- Will set reminders too aggressive and then ignore them. Must be smart about decay.
- Will not fill out detailed profile fields. Must work with just "Name + relationship".
