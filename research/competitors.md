# Competitive landscape

Snapshot: May 2026. The "personal CRM / stay-in-touch" category is more crowded than initial research suggested, with at least 15 named players, but most fall into 2 archetypes and **both have well-known gaps**.

## The two archetypes

### 1. The "networker CRM" (heavy, professional)
- **Dex** — LinkedIn-integrated, auto-syncs jobs, browser extension, mobile + desktop. Built for VCs, BD, founders. Subscription, ~$10/mo billed annually. Common complaints: "painfully slow", "feels like mini Salesforce", "overwhelming for beginners", "expensive". Aimed at people with hundreds of contacts.
- **Clay** (rebranded to **Mesh** after Automattic acquisition, June 2025) — pulls from Gmail/iCloud/LinkedIn, builds a profile of everyone you know. Users say it's *too much* — they're flooded with contacts they don't care about.
- **Covve** — professional networking + scanner.
- **Folk** — CRM for solos and small teams. Not pure consumer.

### 2. The "stay in touch" reminder app (lightweight, but shallow)
- **Garden** — free, simple, custom reminder cadences. Top complaint: "no email or messaging integration". No notes depth.
- **Tend** (multiple apps with this exact name — Relationship Companion, Relationship Garden, Connect) — gamified "tend a garden of friends" metaphor. Cute but feels juvenile to many.
- **Kindred** (multiple — Social, The Private Circle, CRM) — *Kindred: The Private Circle* is closest competitor to what we'd build: on-device, top-3 marking, gift hints. Newer, smaller.
- **Catchup**, **Linc**, **UpHabit**, **Fabriq**, **inTouch** — all variants of reminder + light notes.
- **Monaru** — "being thoughtful, easy" — barely covered in reviews.

## The gap

Both archetypes miss the same thing: **a quiet, beautiful, journal-like place** focused on emotional depth rather than transactional contact-management. The networker tools are too cold. The reminder apps are too thin. Nobody is doing "Day One quality of writing + Things 3 quality of polish + small circle (15 people, not 500)."

**Sweet spot for Linger**: free for ≤7 people, $3.99/mo or $24.99/yr for unlimited + extras. Below Dex's price point. Aligned with calm/journal positioning.

## What's working in the market

- Subscription-based utility apps in this orbit (Bear, Day One, Finch, Sleep Cycle) regularly reach $200K-$500K MRR with single-developer or tiny teams.
- "Calm and minimal" is a real differentiator — Less Annoying CRM and OnePageCRM win solely on "not bloated."
- iOS 26 Liquid Glass is a fresh window: apps that adopt it well will look more current than competitors that haven't migrated.

## What competitors do well that we should match

- **Garden**: dead-simple onboarding, no signup
- **Day One**: writing experience, on-device option, beautiful entry view
- **Things 3**: motion polish, content-first detail view
- **Kindred: The Private Circle**: on-device, no accounts (privacy as a feature)

## What competitors do badly that we should avoid

- **Dex/Clay**: too many fields per contact, networking-flavored UI, anxiety-inducing badges
- **Tend variants**: gamification ("water your friend" = juvenile)
- **Generic CRM ports**: pipeline/stages/leads vocabulary on personal life — feels wrong
