# Features — v2 and beyond

Not for the first launch. Track here so we don't accidentally pull them into v1.

## v1.1 (post-launch, ~3 months after)

- **Voice notes** — record a quick audio memo, transcribed on-device.
- **Photo journal** — attach a photo to a note. Premium gates this.
- **Shortcut integration** — "Hey Siri, log a note about Sarah."
- **Smart rhythm decay** — if you've seen someone 3 times in 2 weeks, automatically suppress nudges for 2 weeks.

## v1.2

- **Calendar integration** — pull from system calendar to auto-detect when you saw someone (with confirmation).
- **Birthday surfacing** — birthdays you've added show in Today 7 days ahead.
- **Person merge** — combine two entries that turned out to be the same person.

## v2.0

- **On-device AI prompts** (Apple Intelligence, fully offline) — "Sarah's surgery was last week — want to check in?"
- **Mac companion app** — same data via iCloud, larger writing surface.
- **Shared families** — for couples or households who share the in-laws/family tree. Optional, opt-in, encrypted multi-user CloudKit zone.

## Maybe (validate first)

- **Gift idea field per person** — could be useful, but risks feeling transactional. Validate with users.
- **Anniversary/important-date pinning** beyond birthdays — adds complexity quickly.
- **iMessage extension** — "log a note about this conversation" from inside iMessage. Cool but Apple regularly breaks these.

## Never

These are off-strategy and should not happen even if requested:

- **LinkedIn / professional networking integration** — Linger is not Dex.
- **Pipeline / stages / leads vocabulary** — wrong emotional register.
- **Social feed of what others are doing** — Linger is private.
- **Group chats or messaging features** — there are 50 apps for this; we're not one.
- **Streaks, points, badges, levels** — gamification breaks the calm tone.
- **AI life coaching / therapist mode** — adjacent product, different team.
- **Web app** — iOS-first identity. Mac maybe, web no.
- **Public profiles / sharing your circle** — direct contradiction of the privacy promise.
